Our Commitment: MacPulse is designed with privacy and security as top priorities. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information to external servers. All communication happens through your private iCloud database.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Data We DO NOT Collect

MacPulse does not collect, store, or transmit any of the following:

  • Personal information (name, email, phone number)
  • Location data
  • Usage analytics or statistics
  • Crash reports or diagnostics
  • Device information beyond what's needed for pairing
  • Browsing history or app usage patterns
  • Contacts or photos
  • Any other personally identifiable information

1.2 Data Stored Locally on Your Device

The following information is stored only on your device using iOS Keychain (encrypted) and UserDefaults:

  • ENCRYPTED Device ID: A randomly generated UUID used to identify paired devices
  • ENCRYPTED Pairing Secrets: Cryptographic keys used to sign and verify commands
  • Paired Device Names: The names of Macs you've paired (e.g., "MacBook Pro")
  • Pairing Timestamps: When devices were paired
  • App Preferences: Whether you've seen the setup guide

Important: This data never leaves your device or iCloud account.

2. How We Use iCloud

MacPulse uses your private iCloud CloudKit database to enable communication between your iPhone and Mac. Here's exactly how it works:

2.1 CloudKit Private Database

  • Scope: All data is stored in YOUR private CloudKit database
  • Access: Only you can access this data - we cannot see it
  • Encryption: Apple encrypts data in transit and at rest
  • Ownership: You own and control all data

2.2 Data Transmitted via iCloud

The only data transmitted through iCloud:

  1. Pairing Requests: During initial device pairing
    • 6-digit pairing code (temporary, expires in 5 minutes)
    • Device ID and name
    • Cryptographic handshake data
  2. Commands: When you send lock/sleep/shutdown commands
    • Command type (lock, sleep, or shutdown)
    • Timestamp
    • HMAC-SHA256 digital signature
    • Device ID
  3. Pairing Revocations: When you unpair devices

All commands are automatically deleted after processing (within 5 minutes) to minimize data retention.

3. Security Measures

MacPulse implements multiple layers of security:

3.1 Command Authentication

  • HMAC-SHA256 Digital signatures on all commands
  • TIMESTAMP VERIFICATION Commands expire after 5 minutes
  • REPLAY PROTECTION Each command can only be executed once
  • RATE LIMITING Maximum 10 commands per minute

3.2 Device Pairing Security

  • Pairing codes are 6 digits (1 million combinations)
  • Codes expire after 5 minutes
  • Single-use codes (can't be reused)
  • Pairing requires physical access to both devices initially
  • Maximum 3 paired devices to prevent abuse

3.3 Data Encryption

  • iOS Keychain encryption for sensitive data
  • Apple's end-to-end iCloud encryption in transit
  • No plain-text storage of secrets

4. Third-Party Services

4.1 Apple Services

MacPulse relies on the following Apple services:

  • iCloud CloudKit: For command transmission (see Section 2)
  • iOS Keychain: For secure local storage

These services are subject to Apple's Privacy Policy.

4.2 No Advertising or Analytics

MacPulse is 100% free with no ads. We do not use:

  • Google Analytics
  • Facebook SDK
  • Advertising networks
  • Crash reporting tools (Crashlytics, Sentry, etc.)
  • Any other third-party analytics or tracking services

5. Data Retention

5.1 On Your Device

  • Pairing data remains until you manually unpair devices
  • App preferences remain until you delete the app
  • Deleting the app removes all locally stored data

5.2 In iCloud

  • Commands are automatically deleted after 5 minutes
  • Pairing requests expire after 5 minutes
  • Pairing responses expire after 10 minutes
  • Revocations expire after 24 hours

6. Your Rights

You have complete control over your data:

6.1 Access

All data stored by MacPulse is accessible to you through:

  • The app's Settings screen (view paired devices)
  • Your iCloud account settings

6.2 Deletion

You can delete your data by:

  • Unpairing devices in the app (removes pairing data)
  • Deleting the MacPulse app (removes all local data)
  • Disabling iCloud for MacPulse in iOS Settings

6.3 Export

Since MacPulse doesn't collect personal information, there's no data to export beyond what's already visible in the app.

7. Children's Privacy

MacPulse is rated 4+ and is safe for all ages. We do not knowingly collect any information from children or anyone else. The app functions the same regardless of the user's age.

8. International Users

MacPulse is available worldwide. All data is stored in:

  • Your device: Wherever you're located
  • Your iCloud account: Apple data centers (subject to Apple's data residency policies)

We do not transfer data to any other countries or jurisdictions.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected by updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page.

Material changes will be communicated through:

  • App Store update notes
  • In-app notification (if significant)
  • This webpage

Continued use of MacPulse after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

10. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA):

  • Legitimate Interest: Processing is necessary for the app to function (device pairing, command transmission)
  • Consent: By using the app, you consent to the minimal data processing described herein

11. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have the right to:

  • Know: What personal information is collected (see Section 1)
  • Delete: Request deletion of personal information (see Section 6.2)
  • Opt-Out: Not applicable - we don't sell personal information

Important: MacPulse collects minimal data, all of which you can delete by unpairing devices or deleting the app.

12. Open Source & Transparency

MacPulse is built with transparency in mind:

  • Security implementation uses industry-standard practices
  • No obfuscation of data handling
  • Minimal permissions requested
  • Source code available upon request for security researchers
