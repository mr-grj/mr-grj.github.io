Frequently Asked Questions

How do I pair my iPhone with my Mac?

Steps:

  1. Install MacPulseHelper on your Mac
  2. Sign into the same iCloud account on both devices
  3. On your Mac, click the MacPulseHelper menu bar icon and select "Pair New Device"
  4. A 6-digit code will appear
  5. Open MacPulse on your iPhone and tap "Pair Device"
  6. Enter the 6-digit code
  7. Wait for confirmation (usually 5-10 seconds)

Tip: For the first pairing, stay near your Mac so you can easily read the code.

Why isn't my Mac responding to commands?

Common solutions:

  • Make sure MacPulseHelper is running on your Mac (check menu bar for the icon)
  • Verify both devices are signed into the same iCloud account
  • Check that you have an active internet connection on both devices
  • Ensure your Mac isn't asleep or powered off
  • Try unpairing and re-pairing your devices
  • Restart MacPulseHelper from the menu bar

How do I grant Accessibility permissions on Mac?

MacPulseHelper needs Accessibility permissions to control your Mac remotely.

Steps:

  1. Open System Settings on your Mac
  2. Go to Privacy & Security â†’ Accessibility
  3. Click the lock icon and enter your Mac password
  4. Find MacPulseHelper in the list and enable the checkbox
  5. If prompted, restart MacPulseHelper from the menu bar

I'm getting "iCloud not available" error

Solutions:

  • Go to Settings â†’ [Your Name] and verify you're signed into iCloud
  • Check that iCloud Drive is enabled
  • Ensure you have an active internet connection
  • Try signing out and back into iCloud (this will re-sync)
  • Wait a few minutes - iCloud sometimes has temporary outages
  • Check Apple's System Status page

The pairing code times out - what should I do?

Pairing codes expire after 5 minutes for security. If you miss the window:

  • Generate a new code on your Mac
  • Make sure you're near your Mac when pairing (for first-time setup)
  • Type the code quickly - you have 5 minutes
  • Check that iCloud is working on both devices
  • Ensure MacPulseHelper is running on your Mac

Can I pair multiple Macs or iPhones?

Multiple Macs: Yes! You can control up to 3 Macs from one iPhone. Just repeat the pairing process for each Mac.

Multiple iPhones: Yes! Each iPhone can be paired independently with your Mac. Just repeat the pairing process for each device.

How do I unpair my devices?

On iPhone:

  1. Open MacPulse
  2. Go to Settings tab
  3. Tap "Unpair All Devices"
  4. Confirm the action

On Mac:

  1. Click MacPulseHelper menu bar icon
  2. Select "Unpair All Devices"
  3. Confirm the action

Note: This will notify the other device that pairing has been revoked.

Is my data secure?

Yes! MacPulse uses industry-standard security:

  • Private iCloud Database: Only you can access your data
  • HMAC-SHA256 Signatures: Military-grade cryptographic signatures
  • Device-Specific Pairing: Unique shared secrets for each device
  • Automatic Cleanup: Commands deleted within 5 minutes
  • No Third Parties: No external servers or analytics
  • Replay Protection: Each command works only once
  • Rate Limiting: Maximum 10 commands per minute
